Creating a Robotic arm control inteface

Robotic arm control inteface

Idea

Main idea is to create an interface for human arm from shoulder to a hand that would cotrol a humanoid robotic arm with 3 joints.

Here are some pictures that would display somehow our idea. But please note that our arm won’t be able to control separated fingers because it would greatly complicate our model.

Inspiration

Inspiration came from multiple futuristic games and films that tried in one way or another to implement an exoskeleton for a part or a whole human body and then control robotic arms huge robots on the distance or sitting inside of them. For example, in the Pacific Rim movie, there were huge robots that fighted a massive Godzilla.

Another more realistic example of usage is surgery. Today robotic arms already help surgeons perform surgeries. But training to use such a machine is difficult and costly. We offer a more intuitive and cheaper solution for the average surgeon, who can put the control interface on his or her hand and immediately perform the necessary actions.

The robotic arm itself can be scaled down, making it much smaller than a human hand and visa versa, it can be scaled up.

The idea is also to make it versatile - it can be attached to a drone, for example, and used in hard-to-reach places or risky operations such as mine clearance.

Robotic arm requirements

That is how the controled robotic should look like to be controlable via our interface:

It would have to have 3 joints:

  1. Shoulder (3 step motors)
  2. Elbow (1 step motor)
  3. Hand (3 step motors)
