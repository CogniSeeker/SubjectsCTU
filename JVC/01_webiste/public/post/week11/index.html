Moulding and casting a small figure and a circuit

Idea

This week we had the opportunity to mould and cast some object that we would bring. I had two models:

  1. A face of the devil made of sculpting plasticine, which my friend kindly lent me (thanks to Matvii).
  2. A circuit board from the watch bracelet I got at the club that could glow in different colors.

Both models are smaller than these dimensions: 5 x 5 x 2 cm.

Also I’ve brought a form that I’ve found at home for the first object. The form for a second object I created myself by using a special cardboard and a sculpting plastiline. We will need them to place an object inside and pour a mold on it.

Moulding

By the way, that is how our working environment looked like:

We took the Dragon Skin (Fast), mixed part A and part B in 1:1 and started to pour in on our objects in forms. Then we waited approx. 30 minutes for the mold to be finally solid so that we could take the object out and pour a cast inside instead of it.

Our teachers also had a silicon form to cast later some mysterious model.

Casting

Now we can take our models back and use the flexible silicon form to create a copy of our model using casting. For this purpose we used Smooth-Cast 305.

After pouring the cast inside our forms, this time we needed to wait longer, approx. for an hour.
