3D Printing

Preparation

This week we learned how to print models created in Fusion360 and sliced in SLicer afterwards. We used PLA filament and Prusa i3 MK3s, Bambulab A1 Mini FDM 3D printers.

We needed to print model that would be max 5cm^3, so it had an impact on my choice. I decided to create a universal watch stand that would suit for my watches.

Model design

Firstly, I measured dimensions of my watch band and designed a model in Fusion360 and tried to do it the largest I could. While designing, I used a Voronoi pattern generator on one side of base stand.

Then I sliced it using PrusaSlicer and got the exact picture how the 3D printer will print

Model printing and cutting

As a result I got this. There are organic supports because it would guarantee the right structure in the weakest parts like Voronoi pattern on the base stand.

Now, I need to clean it cutting the organic supports with small nippers.

Here is the final model I’m proud of.

3D Scanning

We needed to bring some small figure with ourselves to scan it later on specialized machine. I chose a few items:

  1. A watch
  2. A gold medal
  3. a RAM stick
  4. A Queen figure for chess

I didn’t have enough time to scan all of them. Moreover, after I tried to scan my watch I realized that I didn’t work absolutely due to its metal shine. As you may guess, A RAM stick and a gold medal had the same problem, so the best next choice was a Queen.

Here is the result I got:

It was detailed enough, although it had some problematic angles like the top-view angle. Nevertheless, we can even see a light reflection on the figure, which means that scanner recognised it well.
