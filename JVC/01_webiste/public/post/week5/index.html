Creating a LED blinker with changeable frequency

LED blinker with changeable frequency

Goal

This week we soldered a predesigned circuilt “LED blinker” with changeable frequency that depends on the position of the trimmer. Our exercise was to put the components on the Universal Circuit Board as described on this scheme:

Preparation and soldering

First of all, we will lay our components on the board and try to make as many straight connections between its legs as we can. I got something like this.

Also we need to fix components to make it easier to solder later. We will bend the legs of the components to the sides and use a small piece of tape to fix the battery holder.

We will need the following equipment to solder a circult:

  1. A soldering iron heated to 300 degrees
  2. A solder with flux inside
  3. Small wire cutters
  4. Small wires
  5. Tweezers
  6. A holder
  7. A board

I had already some experience in soldering before, so it went smoothly and without noticable problems.

Result

Here is the result from both sides. I needed to use 2 small pieces of wires to create a diagonal wire connection as described on the scheme. I used cut off legs of components to make connections.
